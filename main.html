Breathe clean air...

Be careful


The air we breathe contains many different contaminants that can adversely affect our health. Some of them are instantly dangerous, others cause damage gradually, sometimes over decades. Knowing the dangers of the air we breathe can help prevent serious illness and sometimes sudden death.

Detect danger


Hazardous and harmful substances contained in the inhaled air can be in a solid, liquid or gaseous state. They may have different maximum permissible concentrations, depending on external conditions, the nature of physical activity, exposure time, etc. Some of them cannot be detected without the help of special instruments. There are substances that pose a toxic, carcinogenic or biological hazard even in low concentrations. In some cases, you have to deal with a combination of various dangerous substances that require comprehensive respiratory protection.

Protect yourself


The correct selection of the appropriate personal respiratory protection equipment can only be carried out by specialists, based on accurate data on the state of the air environment. Different respirators provide protection against specific contaminants, the composition and concentration of which is a key determinant of the type of PPE used. In addition to taking into account the protective properties of the respirator, it is required to evaluate its size, compatibility with other elements of individual protection, as well as the fit to the user's face. It is required to carefully study the instructions of the PPE manufacturer and take into account all requirements for its fitting, use, as well as the rules for storage and disposal.
